# AI Workforce Analytics: Hiring, Layoffs & Business Performance

## About the Project

Artificial intelligence is transforming how organizations recruit, manage, and optimize their workforce. This project applies Python-based data analysis techniques to examine hiring patterns, layoffs, AI adoption, automation, employee outcomes, and business performance using a large-scale workforce analytics dataset.

Through data preprocessing, exploratory data analysis, and visualization, the project investigates workforce trends across industries, regions, departments, work models, and varying economic conditions. It also explores relationships among workforce, business, and economic metrics to uncover meaningful insights that can support informed decision-making and strategic workforce planning.

## Key Findings

The analysis revealed that AI adoption increased steadily over time and was strongly associated with automation and moderately associated with productivity. Workforce trends varied across industries, with the AI industry exhibiting the highest hiring and layoff activity, while regional workforce patterns remained relatively consistent. Employee satisfaction was associated with lower attrition, whereas salary and work model showed only weak relationships with employee outcomes. Economic indicators such as GDP growth and inflation exhibited little to no linear relationship with hiring and layoff patterns. Overall, correlation analysis identified a few strong relationships among key business metrics, while most numerical variables demonstrated weak or negligible linear relationships, highlighting the complexity of workforce dynamics.
